Induced Tide

Induced tides \(h_{\rm tides}\) are the height differences that occur due to tidal forces on a planet’s massive ocean’s water level. The values provided are very crued and are only meant as a suggestion to the user. Tide height depends on many local parameters, as explained in artifexian’s video.

For an object of mass \(m\) and radius \(r\) that interacts with a companion object of mass \(m_c\) from a mean distance \(a\) with orbital eccentricity \(e\), will experience a maximum tide height of: \(h_{\rm tides} = 3 \frac{m_c}{m} \left(\frac{r}{a (1 - e)}\right)^3 \frac{r}{2}\).

More information on the model used can be found on https://www.cambridge.org/resources/0521846560/7708_Tidal%20distortion.pdf (Eq. 20) where mean distance is replaced by the periapsis (which yields the maximum observed tides). A mistake must be noted: the example below eq. 20 needs \(m_1\) to be the earth mass and \(m_2\) the moon mass.
