TemperatureÔÉÅ

The surface temperature of an object can be defined differently for objects that produce significant radiation (stars) and ones that are mostly heated by another stellar body (planets, satellites).

For a star of luminosity \(L\) in solar luminosities and radius \(R\) in solar radii, the surface temperature is given by the ideal body equation: \(\frac{L}{R^2} \cdot 5778\) K.

For a planet of bond albedo \(A\), emissivity \(\epsilon\), heat distribution \(\beta\), normalized greenhouse \(g\) and incident flux \(S\), the surface temperature is given by the equation: \(\left(\frac{(1 - A) S}{\beta \epsilon (1 - g)}\right)^{1/4} \cdot 278.5\) K.

For more information of the planetary surface temperature: https://arxiv.org/pdf/1702.07314.pdf (eq. 6 and 16)
